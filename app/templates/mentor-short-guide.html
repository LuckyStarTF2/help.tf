You want to become a mentor

That's awesome! Hopefully you'll find new friends and have a great time helping new players get better at the game. In order to have the best experience for all the parties, there are some rules and advice.

Do not ask for a payment.

That's a rule. Providing services for a reward and helping people out of kindness are different things and cause different expectations and relationships. In case if your apprentice states that they want to pay for your time and effort, it's fine, let them. Just don't ask for it.

Some people may quit.

Don't take it personally if somebody quits TF2 after your lessons. Most likely it's not because you are a bad teacher, but because this is not their cup of tea. You didn't stick to every game you've played, did you?

Be respectful and patient.

Don't expect new players to learn things from the first time. Or even second. Especially when it's something harder than crouch jumping.

It should be obvious, but don't make people feel bad even when they are really bad at something. Encourage and support them. Be a teacher you would like to have.

Fun first, knowledge second.

When somebody just starts playing the game, help them have fun. There's a ton of things to learn in TF2, but don't dump it on a newbie at once. Help them with some crucial settings like FOV and fast weapon switch and just play together. This is not a rule, just an advice. People and their needs are different, I believe you are smart enough to figure out what will be the best for your student :3
